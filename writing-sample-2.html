Paragon

Project Overview

This sample demonstrates my ability to edit lore that informs the design and development of unique characters for a multiplayer game. The goal of "Tales of Agora" was to populate the multiplayer online battle arena with unique characters that the player could take into combat.

Key Deliverables

  • Edited lore for Narbash, a heavy tank with the ability to inspire allies with his drum or disrupt enemies with a melody, all of which has an area of effect that cools down over time.
  • Edited lore for Countess, an assassin with burst cast abilities to execute precision strikes and quick infiltration of enemy positions using misdirection and random moves.
  • Described lore in Shotgun and in person for concept artists and hard surface modelers during the development of Twinblast, Riktor, Iggy and Scorch, Gadget, and Wukong.

Note: As a lore editor, I was not responsible for writing the following excerpts, only to edit the writing to ensure it was consistent with the character design in the world of Agora.

Countess Lore Excerpt

When a provincial noblewoman jumped from a high bridge and hit the water far below, she was found beneath the depths by a dark spirit, and emerged the next morning with cold skin and a strange hunger. She returned to the court that had ended her life and became its sinister ruler as the Countess.

Narbash Lore Excerpt

As the Chosen One of his ogre tribe, Narbash got everything he wanted, until he angered the elders by protesting war with a neighboring tribe. Through inspired drumming, he prevented the war, but was exiled. He took his music across Zechin, making friends everywhere he went.

Conclusion

Effective lore editing requires not just creativity, but the discipline to ensure that the lore is accurately depicting the character's design requirements.
