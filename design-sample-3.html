Resource Management Economy

Project Overview: Balancing the Core Loop

This document details the economic loop for a survival/crafting game, specifically focusing on the acquisition, consumption, and sinks for three tiers of in-game resources. The goal was to maintain a **sustainable and engaging resource scarcity** that drives player progression without creating frustrating grind walls.

Economic Design Pillars

  • **Scarcity:** Resource acquisition should decrease in volume over time to encourage exploration.
  • **Interdependence:** No single resource should be sufficient; crafting must require combinations of different tiers (e.g., Tier 1 Wood + Tier 2 Metal).
  • **Value Decay:** Late-game resources must quickly replace the value of early-game resources to prevent hoarding and obsolescence.

System Implementation Example: Resource Sink

To prevent late-game saturation, a primary resource sink was implemented through the **"Guild Tax"** system. This required a constant, low-level expenditure of rare resources to maintain the player's primary base and status.

The total cost of the Guild Tax rises by 5% for every new structure built after the initial five. This scales the sink with the player's investment, making resource balancing an ongoing challenge, not a solved problem.

$$Tax\ Cost = Base\ Cost \times (1 + 0.05 \times (Structures - 5))$$

Conclusion

Effective game economics rely on detailed mathematical modeling and rigorous playtesting. This sample highlights my proficiency with **spreadsheet analysis and system balancing**, ensuring a fun and challenging progression curve. The full document includes the detailed balancing spreadsheet with all production and consumption rates.
