Level Layout and Flowchart

Project Overview: The Abandoned Processing Center

This document outlines the **player flow, pacing, and encounter design** for a mid-game level focusing on tension and environmental puzzle solving. The core goal was to teach the player a new movement mechanic organically through level geometry.

Design Pillars and Goals

  • **Pacing:** Utilize the layout to alternate between moments of intense combat and quiet exploration.
  • **Player Skill Gate:** Requires mastery of the 'Jump-Dash' mechanic (introduced in a prior level) to bypass obstacles and access hidden paths.
  • **Narrative Integration:** Environmental clues and subtle visual storytelling hint at the fate of the workers who previously occupied the center.

Flowchart Breakdown (Key Junctions)

The level is broken down into five primary zones, detailed in the full flowchart. The critical sequence is as follows:

  1. **Entry:** Linear path, introducing environmental hazard. (Low Intensity)
  2. **Central Atrium:** Wide-open space, optional side path requires **Jump-Dash** for a reward. (Medium Intensity, Discovery Focus)
  3. **Chokepoint Hall:** Required combat encounter using line-of-sight manipulation. (High Intensity)
  4. **Puzzle Room:** Blocked door requiring player to use the environment (and a light puzzle) to progress. (Low Intensity, Puzzle Focus)
  5. **Exit:** Boss/Mini-Boss arena. (Climax)

Conclusion

The final layout emphasizes **legibility and intentionality**. Every space is designed to reinforce a specific gameplay mechanic or emotional beat, ensuring no area feels like wasted space. The full document includes top-down schematics and detailed encounter scripting.
